Fifth Third Bank Project

Geodemographic Analysis & Competitive Benchmarking

Project Overview

This project exemplified what I love most: collaborating with a high-performing, interdisciplinary team to extract signal from complex data, translate that into strategic business value, and communicate insights through both predictive models and visual storytelling. Due to the nature of the engagement, specific project details are protected under a non-disclosure agreement (NDA).

At the Institute for Advanced Analytics, I had the opportunity to collaborate with Fifth Third Bank on a high-impact project focused on geodemographic segmentation and competitive benchmarking across the U.S. financial landscape. Working with over 70,000 observations and 1,300+ demographic and behavioral features stored in PostgreSQL, we designed a scalable, end-to-end machine learning pipeline that empowered the bank to better understand its customer base and market position.

Our team executed a two-phase data science workflow. In Phase 1, we developed unsupervised learning models using KMeans, DBSCAN, BIRCH, and k-NN to cluster customer profiles and uncover geospatial patterns. These insights revealed the bank’s core consumer segments and identified regional competitors with similar customer compositions, informing strategic expansion and customer acquisition plans.

In Phase 2, we engineered geospatial and domain-specific features that significantly improved signal quality for downstream modeling. We then implemented and evaluated a suite of supervised learning algorithms—including XGBoost, neural networks, explainable boosting machines (EBMs), and logistic regression—to forecast predict performance and identify high-potential growth zones.

We synthesized these insights into an interactive Tableau dashboard tailored for both technical and business stakeholders, enabling leadership to quickly identify target markets, understand competitive saturation, and make data-driven decisions with confidence.
